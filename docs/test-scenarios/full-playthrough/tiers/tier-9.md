# Tier 9 — conversion, quantum, and Phase 5

Set tier to 9; unlock alts ≤ T9 (everything). The endgame: the Converter
and SAM chain go live, the Quantum Encoder's excited-matter loops arrive,
and the run closes with Space Elevator Phase 5.

## Unlocks (per library data)

- **Buildings:** Converter, Quantum Encoder
- **Belts:** Mk6 — 1200/min
- **Recipes:** ore conversion (Converter), Ficsite Ingot ×3 + Ficsite
  Trigon, Time Crystal, Dark Matter Residue/Crystal, Diamonds, Excited
  Photonic Matter, Neural-Quantum Processor, Superposition Oscillator,
  AI Expansion Server, Singularity Cell, Ballistic Warp Drive,
  Biochemical Sculptor, Ficsonium + Ficsonium Fuel Rod, Synthetic Power
  Shard, Alien Power Matrix

## Build objectives

1. **SAM Conversion Works** (new, at a SAM claim) — Reanimated SAM →
   Ficsite Ingot (pick the cheapest source per the optimizer — it chose
   converter-made bauxite in earlier testing; verify and record) →
   Ficsite Trigon 15/min. Planning a Ficsite target must auto-force the
   SAM toggle on; if it doesn't, showstopper bug.
2. **Quantum Lab** (new or Quartz Electronics annex) — Time Crystal
   3/min, Dark Matter Crystal 3/min, Diamonds 7.5/min, Excited Photonic
   Matter loop, Superposition Oscillator 1/min, Neural-Quantum Processor
   0.75/min, AI Expansion Server 0.5/min. Excited-matter/dark-matter
   loops are reuse-edge territory — same scrutiny as aluminum water.
3. **Warp Drive Final** (Elevator Yard) — Singularity Cell 2/min,
   Ballistic Warp Drive 0.5/min, Biochemical Sculptor 0.5/min (Blender;
   imports from the aluminum + quantum factories). **Phase 5: AI
   Expansion Server + Ballistic Warp Drive + Biochemical Sculptor.**
4. **Ficsonium loop** (Nuclear annex) — Ficsonium 1/min + Ficsonium Fuel
   Rod 0.5/min, eating the plutonium waste stream; restate the waste
   ledger from Tiers 7–8.
5. **Converter ore patches** — use ore conversion to plug at least one
   real shortage (e.g. bauxite from copper) instead of a new claim;
   record the optimizer's pick and its raw cost.
